# Serenity-UAV

## Can't stop the signal and can't take the sky from me

A functional, security-conscious Unmanned Aerial Vehicle based on the Firefly-class spacecraft
Serenity from the 2002 show. Designed using Claude AI.

## Specifications

**Overall:** 24.0" L × 19.1" W × 7.2" H (609 × 486 × 182 mm)

**Hull:** misubisu — "Serenity Firefly with landing gear and swivel engines"
[thingiverse.com/thing:7330462](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:7330462) — scaled to 24", hollowed, 2 lb/ft³ closed-cell foam fill.
Shell: 2.5 mm PLA walls. STLs generated by `blender_shells_v3.py`.

**Propulsion (all 6S — Rev O/P baseline):**

- 2 nacelles (port + starboard), each: 2 × 50 mm EDFs in tandem series — ~911 g thrust per nacelle, ~1822 g combined
- Counter-rotating EDF pairs: port nacelle CW from intake, starboard CCW — zero net torque reaction
- 11-fin twisted inter-stage stator per nacelle, integral to nacelle CF-PETG print. 33° vane angle.
  Generated by `blender_nacelle_revo.py` / `nacelle_pod_50mm_tandem.scad`.
- 1 × 120 mm EDF in the rear fuselage compartment — ~3500 g thrust, exhaust straight aft
- Total thrust: ~5322 g | T/W ≈ 1.47 at 6S 4000 mAh (~3608 g AUW with Rev P cargo hardware)

**Nacelle tilt (Rev O — CG pivot):** 0° (cruise / horizontal) → 90° (hover / vertical) → 120° (backing thrust).
Hard stops at −5° and 140°. Pivot relocated to Z=83 mm (nacelle CG) — eliminates gravity torque on servo at all tilt angles.
One digital servo (≥25 kg·cm @ 6V) per nacelle, fuselage-mounted.

**Variable-area nozzles (Rev O — M=1.0 gear train):** 8-petal iris on all 3 EDF exits.

- Nacelle nozzles (2×): gear-linked passively to the tilt pivot — no dedicated servo.
  0° nacelle tilt = nozzle fully closed; 90° tilt = nozzle fully open (Serenity at full burn).
  Gear train: M=1.0 sector R=22 mm → pinion N=12T → bevel pair N=14T 45° → crown N=12T → ring rack R=28 mm → ~70.7° ring travel for 90° nacelle sweep.
- Rear nozzle (1×): servo actuated (SG90 class). 8 moving petals + 8 fixed structural ribs
  integral to `rear_nozzle_frame.stl`.
- Total servos: 5 (2 nacelle tilt + 1 rear nozzle + 2 cargo door/release — Rev P).
- Inner petal face: translucent-blue PETG, backlit by WS2812B LED ring inside each duct.
- Iris mechanism concept: [Variable-area EDF nozzle by BamJr](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:2991269)
  (CC BY 4.0) — all Rev O/P nozzle geometry original.

Fuselage EDF intake: 4 radial scoops at neck station ~310 mm via `s_neck_intake_frame.stl` +
`s_aft_edf_plenum.stl` (cross-shaped 4-to-1 plenum manifold). Replaces belly scoop from earlier revisions.

**Avionics (PocketBeagle 2 Industrial — 8-node cooperative architecture):**

8 × PocketBeagle 2 Industrial (AM6254) boards — 4 with Cape-A (sensor/flight capes: GPS, IMU,
barometer, airspeed, FPV camera, TPM 2.0, ADC, ESC telemetry, PWM, GPIO) and 4 with Cape-B
(comms/logging capes: MAVLink/SiK 915 MHz, LoRa RFM95W 915 MHz, TI WL1837MOD WiFi/BT, 49 MHz
TDDS transceiver via RCRS-49 sub-module, CAN FD, MIL-STD-1553B, RS-485, Ethernet RSTP ring, TPM 2.0,
ATF16V8BQL CPLD write-blocker, hardware-enforced non-executable log microSD).

**Cargo handling (Rev P — complete):** clamshell cargo bay with CF-PETG port/starboard doors,
8-barrel piano hinge on 3 mm CF rod, SG90 door servo + SG90 release servo via DRV8833 H-bridge,
N20 winch motor + Dyneema SK75 0.5 mm line, auto-latch payload cradle, HX711 load cell, FPV camera
bezel, GPS retention ring, 3M foam gasket door seal. Payload capacity 400 g.
