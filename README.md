# Serenity-UAV

## Can't stop the signal and can't take the sky from me

A functional, security-conscious Unmanned Aerial Vehicle based on the Firefly-class spacecraft
Serenity from the 2002 show. Designed using Claude AI.

## Specifications

**Overall:** 24.0" L × 19.1" W × 7.2" H (609 × 486 × 182 mm)

**Hull:** misubisu — "Serenity Firefly with landing gear and swivel engines"
[thingiverse.com/thing:7330462](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:7330462) — scaled to 24", hollowed, 2 lb/ft³ closed-cell foam fill.
Shell: 2.5 mm PLA walls. STLs generated by `blender_shells_v3.py`.

**Propulsion (all 6S):**

- 2 × 50 mm EDFs in series per nacelle (4 total) — ~911 g thrust per nacelle, ~1823 g combined
- 1 × 120 mm EDF in the rear fuselage compartment — ~3500 g thrust, exhaust straight aft
- Total thrust: ~5300 g | T/W ≈ 1.29 at 750 g battery
- 8-fin inter-stage stator between EDF pair in each nacelle — straightens swirl, ~6% thrust gain.
  33° vane angle (matched to 50 mm EDF tip swirl at 6S). Generated by `blender_stator_gen.py`. Print × 4.

**Variable-area nozzles (Rev L — all-new geometry):** 8-petal iris on all 5 EDF exits.
Closed: petals form the hull-skin continuation of the nacelle tip / rear engine bell —
exterior matches Serenity's engine cones exactly as modelled.
Open: petals hinge radially outward, exposing the EDF bore and replicating the
"Serenity at full burn" look from the show (inner petal face painted/printed translucent
blue, backlit by WS2812B LED ring inside each duct).
Actuation: one SG90-class servo per nozzle via piano-wire pushrod to an internal link ring.
Generated by `blender_nozzle_gen.py` from measured hull cross-sections.
Iris mechanism concept: [Variable-area EDF nozzle by BamJr](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:2991269)
(Creative Commons — Attribution) — all exterior geometry is original Rev L work.

Fuselage EDF intake: cut into s\_middle underside (Z=0 face) at the aft transition of the
flat-belly section, 120 mm diameter, centred laterally.
STL: `s_middle_intake_shell24.stl`. Generated by `blender_intake_cut.py`.

**Avionics (Revision K — Beagleboard ecosystem):**

8 × Pocket Beagle 2 boards, 4 with sensor capes (GPS, IMU, barometer, airspeed, FPV camera)
and 4 with comms capes (MAVLink/LoRa 915 MHz, 49 MHz TDDS transceiver, CAN FD, MIL-STD-1553,
RS-485, Ethernet, TPM). All nodes connected via COMMS-HAT-SWITCH with hardware-enforced
non-executable log storage and a forensically sound write blocker on the recorder TF slot.

**Cargo handling:** payload release, winch motor + drum, navigation and anti-collision lights.
